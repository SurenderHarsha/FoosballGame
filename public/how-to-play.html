How to Play GoalNet

Everything you need to know to start playing

1. Getting Started

  1. Open the game in your browser.
  2. Choose 2 Players (1v1) or 4 Players (2v2).
  3. Type a Room Code — any word or name. Share this exact code with your friend(s).
  4. Click Join Game. Wait for your opponent(s) to join the same room.
  5. Once everyone is in, the game starts with a 3-second countdown.
  6. Click the table to lock your mouse. This is required to play.
Tip

In 4-player mode, each team of 2 splits the rods: one player controls GK + DEF, the other controls MID + ATK.

2. The Table

The table has 8 rods arranged in real foosball order, alternating between Blue and Red teams:

  1. Blue GK (1 man) — Goalkeeper
  2. Blue DEF (2 men) — Defense
  3. Red ATK (3 men) — Attacker
  4. Blue MID (5 men) — Midfield
  5. Red MID (5 men) — Midfield
  6. Blue ATK (3 men) — Attacker
  7. Red DEF (2 men) — Defense
  8. Red GK (1 man) — Goalkeeper

Blue scores by getting the ball into the right goal. Red scores by getting it into the left goal. First to 10 goals wins.

3. Controls

All controls use the mouse and keyboard. You must click the table first to lock your mouse (Pointer Lock). Press Esc to unlock.

Rod Selection

You have 4 rods. Select which rod to move using QWER:

QGoalkeeper (GK) WDefense (DEF) EMidfield (MID) RAttacker (ATK)

The selected rod is highlighted in gold on the table.

Dual Rod Mode

Hold two keys at once (e.g. Q + W) to control two rods simultaneously. Both rods move together with the mouse. Advanced players use this to coordinate defense and attack.

Moving a Rod

Mouse Up/DownSlides the selected rod vertically

Move your mouse up or down while it's locked to slide the rod. The men on the rod move with it. DEF rods have extra slide range.

Kicking

Left ClickForward kick (toward enemy goal) Right ClickBack kick (away from enemy goal)

When you click, the selected rod swings and hits the ball. The kick has two properties controlled by your mouse movement:

  • Power: Move your mouse faster before clicking = stronger kick. The power bar in the bottom-right corner shows your current power level (green = weak, red = strong).
  • Angle: Flick your mouse left or right before clicking to aim the shot at an angle. The arrow indicator shows your current angle. No flick = straight shot.
How to Shoot Hard

Move your mouse quickly in any direction, then immediately click. The faster the mouse was moving, the harder the shot. Practice quick flick-and-click combos.

Ball Trapping (Pinning)

When a slow ball approaches your rod, you can trap it by moving the rod gently. This requires skill:

  • Too still (not moving the mouse): Ball bounces off your player — no pin.
  • Sweet spot (gentle, continuous movement): Ball sticks to your player. Keep subtle mouse movements going.
  • Too fast (jerky movement): Ball deflects wildly.

Trapping lets you set up a controlled shot instead of just deflecting.

Other Controls

TRequest table shake (both teams must press T to shake a stuck ball) EscUnlock mouse (pause your control)

4. Special Moves

Each rod position has unique special moves. These activate only when the ball is touching the rod. The moves panel below the table shows which moves are available (highlighted gold when usable).

How to Use Special Moves

  1. Select the rod with Q W E R
  2. Make sure the ball is touching your rod's players
  3. Press the move's key: F G H J for role-specific moves
  4. Universal moves: V for Spinneroni, B for Fake Shot, BB for Dribble Shot

Hover over any move in the panel to see a detailed tooltip explaining controls and skill requirements.

Important

Special moves only activate when the ball is literally touching the rod. If the ball is even slightly away, the move won't fire. Position carefully.

5. Tips for Beginners

  • Start with one rod. Don't try dual-rod mode until you're comfortable with single-rod control.
  • Defense first. Select DEF (W) and learn to block shots before trying to attack.
  • Gentle mouse = better trapping. Practice the sweet spot. It's the foundation of ball control.
  • Power comes from speed. A fast mouse flick right before clicking gives a powerful shot.
  • Back kicks are useful. Right-click to kick backwards when you need to reset or pass between rods.
  • Watch the power bar. Bottom-right of the table. Green = gentle, Red = full power.
  • Fake shots (B) cost nothing and can trick opponents. Use them often.
  • Table shake (T) requires both teams to agree. Use it when the ball is stuck.
